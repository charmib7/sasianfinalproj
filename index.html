WAR DECLARED
The Cousins to Meet on the Battlefield — Kingdoms Across the Lands Choose Sides

In a development that shocks no one who has been paying attention for the past fourteen years, the Pandava brothers and the Kaurava princes have formally declared war, setting the sacred plains of Kurukshetra as their battlefield. The two great armies are expected to meet at dawn.

The Pandavas stand under the command of Dhrishtadyumna of Panchala, with the great Arjuna, Wolfbelly Bhima, the twin warriors Nakula and Sahadeva, Satyaki of the Vrishnis, and Shikhandi of Panchala among its prominent champions. The Kaurava army is led by the venerable Bhishma, grandsire of both houses, alongside Drona, Ashvatthama, Kripacharya, Shalya, and Jayadratha of Sindhu.

Readers may note the conspicuous absence of Karna from active duty. Coverage following.

"He chose the man over the million." — On Arjuna's decision

Among the most discussed decisions today, is when Lord Krishna offered the two sides a choice — his army of one million soldiers or Krishna himself. Furthermore, Krishna would abstain from fighting. Arjuna was offered the choice first and without hesitation, he chooses Krishna as his charioteer. "I know you could win this war single-handed, and so could I. With you steering my chariot, we shall gain great glory together. Best of men, may we prevail!" Duryodhana, on the other hand was exultant to recieve an army of a million of the strongest warriors. Analysts across the subcontinent continue to debate who got the short end of the stick.

FIELDS EMPTIED, STABLES STRIPPED

Across the countryside, recruiters from both camps have swept through villages and farms, pressing men into service. Farmhands who last week tended rice paddies now sharpen spears. Granaries are requisitioned. Families watch the roads.

The beasts of war assemble in numbers not seen in living memory: horses trained to charge in tight formation; fighting elephants schooled to trample into the thick of battle; and innumerable beasts hauling across the dust of the plain toward Kurukshetra.

Internal Disputes
And their reasons for refusing to fight

BHISHMA & SHIKHANDIN: The grandsire Bhishma, commanding the Kaurava forces, has announced one condition his own generals find deeply peculiar: he will not raise his bow against Shikhandi of Panchala because Shikhandin was, in a previous life, a woman. Born Amba, princess of Kashi, she was wronged by Bhishma in her first life and returned reborn, sworn to destroy him. Bhishma knows this and will not fight Shikhandin.

The Pandavas are placing Shikhandi at the very front of their formation. Bhishma is reported to be aware of this. Both parties appear to have accepted the logic of the arrangement.

KARNA & BHISHMA: The great Karna, Duryodhana's most valued champion, has refused to fight so long as Bhishma holds command. The enmity between the two men is bitter: Bhishma often undermines Karna's prowess due to his social status. Karna refuses to serve under the man who diminshes him. Instead, the moment Bhishma falls, Karna will take the field.

BALARAMA: Having taught both men personally, Balarama declared himself unable in conscience to take up arms against his own students. His departure reflects his brother Krishna's, who chooses to have a non-combatant role on the Pandava side as an unarmed charioteer."

✦ Celebrations ✦
WEDDING OF THE SEASON
Prince Abhimanyu Weds Princess Uttaraa
— ❧ —

In what guests are calling the calm before the storm, the marriage of young Abhimanyu — son of Arjuna and Subhadra of Dvaraka — to Princess Uttaraa of Virata was celebrated with great ceremony at the Virata palace.

The groom, trained personally by Lord Krishna, is widely considered the most promising warrior of his generation. He reportedly learned the Chakravyuha formation while still in his mother's womb, though sources suggest he may have missed the exit strategy.

The bride wore silk. The groom wore armour. The guests were mostly warriors. It was, by all accounts, a beautiful occasion.

WHAT WERE THE PANDAVAS REALLY DOING IN VIRATA?
Sources: Several Palace Servants, One Gossipy Maid
YUDHISHTHIRA — The man who is famously untalented at gambling took up residence in King Virata's gambling hall as a dice expert. The irony has not been lost on this correspondent. He called himself "Kanka."
BHIMA — Worked as a royal chef. Sources confirm the food was extraordinary. He also killed the court wrestling champion in his spare time, which raised some eyebrows. He claimed that the man merely tripped.
ARJUNA — Arjuna, the greatest archer alive, spent a year teaching music and dance to Princess Uttaraa. He wore women's clothing, eyeliner, bangles (some say to cover up bruises from his bow), and went by "Brhannala.".
NAKULA & SAHADEVA — Horse groomer and cowherd, respectively. The twins have refused all comment. Can't say we blame them.
DRAUPADI — Served as lady's maid to Queen Sudeshna. The people are outraged. The Queen of Indraprastha as a maid. It's unthinkable. Imagine the hardships she had to go through, not to mention the harassments by Keechaka.
✦ Investigative Report ✦
KARNA: WHO IS HE, REALLY?
The Warrior, The Mystery, The Armour

He is the greatest warrior on the Kaurava side. While this may be controversial, some claim he is the greatest warrior alive, surpassing Arjuna! He is Duryodhana's most trusted companion, but almost nobody knows where he comes from.

Born to a charioteer — or so the official story goes — Karna arrived at the Kuru princes' exhibition tournament and outperformed Arjuna in every discipline. When asked his lineage, he could not answer. Kripacharya barred him from competing. But Duryodhana saw the potential and made him King of Anga on the spot, sealing their friendship forever.

But whispers persist. He was born with divine armour fused to his skin and earrings of celestial gold. No charioteer's son is born so. The sun god Surya is mentioned in hushed tones. And why, this paper asks, has Kunti (mother of the Pandavas) been seen weeping alone after looking at him?

All the evidence leaves people theorizing about his true origin. Could this man be of Kshatriya lineage?
